CITY OVERVIEW

Odense, is Denmark's third-largest city, known for its rich history as the birthplace of fairy-tale author Hans Christian Andersen. The city features charming cobblestone streets, vibrant cultural attractions, and beautiful parks. The city's blend of historical charm and modern amenities, along with its picturesque surroundings, creates an inviting atmosphere for both residents and visitors alike.

The city boasts medieval architecture, including Odense Cathedral and the historic Odense Castle. Its culture celebrates literature, art, and music, with annual festivals and events, reflecting a blend of traditional Danish heritage and contemporary creativity.

New Hans Christian Andersen Museum

ARCHITECTURAL FEATURES

New Hans Christian Andersen Museum, plays a core role in the new Urban-scape of Odense city. The museum reconnects the historic and modern parts of the city by transforming an arterial road into a public space. Situated between traditional wooden houses and modern urban development, the design reflects the duality present in Andersen's work, highlighting contrasts like reality vs. imagination and nature vs. manmade elements.

The museum features a series of interconnected circular forms, organized in a non-hierarchical manner. A continuous green wall weaves through the site, blurring the lines between indoor and outdoor spaces. Exhibition areas are located underground, while a captivating garden above, framed by curved hedges, extends the museum experience. Visitors navigate through a maze-like landscape, where sunken gardens serve as portals between Andersen's fairytale world and reality.

ACTIVITIES

At the new Hans Christian Andersen Museum, visitors can explore immersive exhibitions showcasing Andersen's life and fairy tales, engage with interactive displays, and stroll through enchanting gardens. The museum offers workshops, cultural events, and guided tours, allowing guests to experience the magic of Andersen's stories in a captivating environment.
