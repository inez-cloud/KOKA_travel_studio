V&A DUNDEE – Scotland's Design Museum

CITY OVERVIEW

Dundee, located on the east coast of Scotland, is known for its rich industrial heritage and vibrant cultural scene. The city features historic landmarks like the V&A Museum and the RRS Discovery.

This city has a rich history as a major textile and shipbuilding hub during the Industrial Revolution. It is famed for its jute production and the discovery of the Antarctic by Captain Scott. The city boasts a vibrant cultural scene, featuring the Dundee Contemporary Arts center and the annual Dundee Flower and Food Festival, celebrating its artistic and culinary heritage.

V&A DUNDEE – Scotland's Design Museum

ARCHITECTURAL FEATURES

V&A Dundee elegantly faces the River Tay, projecting over the water to blend seamlessly with the natural environment. Inspired by the rugged cliffs of Orkney Island, the design features stacked layers of precast concrete slabs at varying angles, creating a dynamic façade with subtle nuances. A central void connects the museum to Union Street, revitalizing the relationship between the city and the river.

The interior is designed with randomly attached panels, creating a spacious and inviting atmosphere. The upward expansion of the space offers a unique openness, setting it apart from traditional museum foyers. This versatile area hosts concerts and performances, making the V&A a communal hub for the people of Dundee.

ACTIVITIES

At the V&A Museum in Dundee, you can explore innovative exhibitions showcasing design, art, and culture. Enjoy guided tours, participate in workshops, and attend lectures or performances. The museum's café offers refreshments, while its stunning architecture and waterfront views provide a perfect backdrop for relaxation and inspiration.
