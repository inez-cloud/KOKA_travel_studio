ISHIKAWA

CERABO KUTANI Ceramic Laboratory

CITY OVERVIEW

Ishikawa, on the western coast of Honshu by the Sea of Japan, has a rich history as a center for traditional crafts, particularly Kutani pottery and Kaga textiles. The region is known for its historical sites, including Kanazawa Castle and Kenrokuen Garden. Its culture blends ancient practices with modern influences, celebrating festivals, culinary traditions, and a vibrant arts scene.

CERABO KUTANI Ceramic Laboratory

ARCHITECTURAL FEATURES

Cerabo Kutani, is a ceramics museum shows visitors the process of making Kutani porcelain. This museum is able to show the depth of craftsmanship in Japan by carrying on the functions of clay- making and combining them with the functions of a museum.

The building is connected by a multi-function roof like it is a landform, creating a seamless connection between nature, art, and architecture. The interior and exterior walls were made by the Hanasaka clay from this area with carbon fiber as wall substrates to reproduce traditional tea-ceremony room construction methods in a contemporary style. It harmoniously blends traditional Kutani pottery aesthetics with modern design principles.

ACTIVITIES

At Cerabo Kutani, you can experience traditional Kutani pottery-making through workshops and demonstrations. Explore the gallery showcasing exquisite ceramics, learn about the history and techniques of this art form, and shop for unique handcrafted pieces. The venue often hosts cultural events, enhancing your appreciation of Japanese craftsmanship and design.
